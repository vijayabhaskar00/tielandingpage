Frequently Asked Questions


What is a TiE Grad premier leauge(GPL)?

GPL is a four-stage contest that is open to all final-year CSE/IT/ECE students. The preliminary stage will be conducted in the college and the candidates clearing that stage will move onto the subsequent stages leading to a Hackathon.


What are the four stages?

1. Stumagz - MCQ Quiz
2. CodeTantra - Coding
3. Perspect.AI- Behaviour
4. Grad Premier League


Do I have to be an engineering student?

Yes. We will accept applications from all departments. It is sufficient to have an interest in this subject. However, we expect you to have the technical qualifications to construct your ideas.

If you are a tech-geek who loves to code to solve problems, this is how you can start solving them!


What is the language of the contest?

The Hackathon and its evaluation processes will be in English.


Why Join?

It is to be TiE Hyderabad’s first connected hacker league! An exceptional opportunity to come together with other talents/students in a challenge of international scope, sharing ideas and experimenting with the latest technologies and innovations.


Who can apply?

The hackathon is open to those students in TiE Partner colleges who are interested in technology and innovation and have an interesting project or other idea to develop within the scope of the challenges proposed.Participation in the hackathon is open to individuals aged 18 or older.


How to form a team?

In the first four stages the students are requested to perform individually, Participation is open only to Bidding charter members


How to apply?

Sign up on our event page at [link].


Who judges the winners?

The winning team will be decided by a jury made up of industry experts and professionals of different corporations.


What are the evaluation criteria for the selection of the winning team

The jury will analyze and evaluate all works submitted by the team according to the following four evaluation criteria: Concept, Design, Development ,Presentation


What is the theme for the hackathon?

The main theme of the hackathon is, “Connect, Collaborate, Create.” Participants will be asked to use digital technologies in creative, innovative ways to benefit people and the environment with a view to offering solutions in a range of fields. The goal of this challenge is to create prototypes of products, services and ideas that are able to generate social and economic value for the community by hacking common objects and using open data, open source libraries, public APIs, frameworks for fast prototyping and software development, microcontrollers, sensors, and other hardware (e.g. 3D printers, beacons, wearables, etc.). Sub-Themes of the hackathon are Opportunity, Mobility and Sustainability.


What are the awards?

Winning teams will be awarded to the following prizes by the panel of judges:
1 st place team: $5000 USD

I am an intern/employee working at Accenture. Can I attend the contest?

No ,employees or other collaborators of any companies may compete in the hackathon


What should I bring with me for the GPL?

Contestants must bring their own computers, ID cards and other tools they may need (charging device, mouse, keyboard, USB flash drive, etc).


Do we need to be at the contest area during the whole activity?

Yes. You must be at the contest area


What will be provided during the contest?

Free wi-fi access, Two-day food and beverages, Mentors for groups. Welcome kit.


Does GRAD Premier League help with travel reimbursements?

Unfortunately, GRAD premier Leauge isn’t able to provide hackers with travel reimbursements. We have in certain cases provided it in the past but we’re always on the hunt to find more sustainable ways to bring more students to hackathons.


I just graduated, can I still come to an event?

No, Students from TiE Partner Colleges can only attend GPL as this is is a tie exclusive for partner colleges


What if I don’t know anybody there?

Not to worry! You’ll meet people when you get there. Everybody’s very friendly. A lot of people go to hackathons without teams. So it’s perfectly normal to go around to people, tell them you’re looking for a team, and ask if you can help out. Hackathons will often run team-matching sessions at the start to help you meet people
